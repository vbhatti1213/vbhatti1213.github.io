Brand Repositioning — Strategic Overview

From payday stigma
to purpose-led ally

Cashco Financial · 2019–2023 · Vaishally Bhatti, Director Brand & Insights

The BHAG

"Put 1 Million Canadians on a Pathway to Financial Health."

Social impact positioning. Cashco was built on a founder's belief that financial services should offer a hand up, not a handout — that people navigating financial hardship deserve dignity, real tools, and a genuine pathway forward. The repositioning brought that belief to the surface: from transactional lender to purpose-led brand grounded in real stories and real impact. Our promise was simple — Hope today. Relief for tomorrow. Every brand decision, product experience, and community program was built to make that promise felt.

The Transformation — From / To

The old narrative
The Cashco way
Payday loans
Wage advances that respect your dignity
Short-term goals only
Long-term financial health as the destination
50-30-20 budgets (built for Prime earners)
80-10-10 budgets — needs, good spend, splurge spend
6-month emergency savings as the benchmark
1-month emergency savings as a real starting point
"Prime" credit scores as the only measure of worth
A new credit model not correlated to income
Bankruptcy as stigma
Bankruptcy as reset — credit consolidation as a pathway forward
Buying a house as the only housing goal
Rental housing security as a legitimate, worthy milestone
Comprehensive monthly budgets that overwhelm
1 or 2 category weekly budgets that actually stick

Brand Pillars

01
Real World Stories
Client journeys — not stock photography and aspirational copy. Authentic voices from people who had been where our clients were.
02
Empathy as a Superpower
Front-line teams trained to meet financial shame and guilt with curiosity and care — not judgment. Every interaction is a brand moment.
03
The Pathway, Not the Product
Every touchpoint — from ad to approval to recovery — reinforced a single idea: this is the beginning of something better, not a last resort.
04
Financial Literacy as Brand
Education wasn't a CSR add-on. It was woven into the product experience — proving that Cashco's interest was in clients graduating, not staying.

Financial Literacy Programs Built

Wealthing Like Rabbits — Customized Edition
Co-developed a Cashco-branded version of the national bestseller tailored to alternative lending clients and their real financial starting points.
The Bunny's Talk Money Book
Original financial literacy book for ages 5–8. Built to start money conversations at home — because financial shame starts early.
80-10-10 Budget Framework
A simplified budgeting model designed for clients who can't sustain traditional budget structures — needs, intentional spend, and joy spend.
Credit Pathway Program
A staged credit-building model that rewarded milestones rather than penalized starting points — decoupling creditworthiness from income.
