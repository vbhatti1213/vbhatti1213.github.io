Cashco Brand Repositioning — Legacy Lender to Purpose-Led Ally
Brand Strategy

Cashco Financial · 2019–2023

Cashco had strong community roots but a brand that read transactional. I repositioned it as Alberta's most trusted alternative lender — developing brand playbooks, client experience frameworks, and a full identity system that tied design principles to client journey touchpoints. This wasn't a logo refresh. It was a culture shift: from ad to app to recovery, every experience had to feel consistent with the brand promise.

Outcome Client sentiment scores above 90%. Loan book grew to $40M. Double-digit growth within months of repositioning launch.

Sample — Internal Brand Manifesto (excerpt)

Who we are — and who we're for. We exist for the person who got a "no" when they needed a "yes." Not because they made bad choices. Because the system wasn't designed for their life — a gap in employment, a credit file that doesn't reflect who they are now, a moment when timing and circumstance didn't align. We don't pretend we're a bank. We're something different — and we think that's a strength. Our job is to meet people where they are, help them get where they're going, and make sure the experience of working with us feels like the beginning of something better — not a last resort. Every touchpoint. Every interaction. Every approval, and every hard conversation. That's the brand.
The Pathway Report — Financial Inclusion Thought Leadership
Thought Leadership

Cashco Financial · 2024

I authored Cashco's client insight publication during a pivotal year — a $50M capital investment that demanded clear, credible positioning. The Pathway Report translated behavioural and sentiment data from 150K+ Canadians into a national conversation about financial exclusion, reshaping how leadership thought about brand, mission, and market opportunity. The Pathway Report positioned Cashco as a thought leader, not just a lender, and earned media coverage and speaking invitations in the alternative finance space. I owned concept development, stakeholder buy-in, editorial strategy, design direction, and led distribution strategy.

Outcome National visibility for Cashco's mission. Thought-leadership credibility across the financial inclusion space. First publication of its kind in the alternative lending category in Canada.

Sample — Executive Summary Opening

The Pathway Report: What 150,000 Canadians Taught Us About Financial Exclusion Most financial institutions measure success in approvals. We started measuring it in pathways — the journeys our clients take from financial crisis to stability, and what actually makes those journeys shorter. Over two years, we analyzed behavioural patterns, sentiment data, and milestone outcomes across our client base. What we found challenges the conventional story about alternative lending. Our clients are not financial failures. They are people navigating gaps that the mainstream system wasn't built to handle — gaps in credit history, in documentation, in timing. When the right product meets the right moment, something changes: habits shift, credit improves, and dependence on high-cost credit shrinks. This report documents what that looks like, and what it means for the future of financial inclusion in Canada.

MoneyUp — Building a Challenger Brand from Concept to MVP
Product & Innovation

I was seconded to build a digital-first, mobile-first challenger to the payday loan model. Starting with customer discovery and engaging with 50+ clients, I drove concept to MVP — owning brand identity, GTM strategy, brand pillars, and Brand and Style Guides. As a key stakeholder alongside Product and Technology, I shaped client flow design, UX/UI, and in-app support across onboarding, payments, and retention — informed by real-time client insights. This was a zero-to-one proof of concept for what inclusive digital lending could look like — built to disrupt a category that had never been seriously challenged on experience.

Outcome 30% reduction in client onboarding time. 4.8-star early Google review average. 15% improvement in user retention. 10% faster activation post-redesign.

Sample — Brand Brief, MoneyUp

MoneyUp — Brand Brief The opportunity: The payday loan space has one experience: transactional, rushed, and designed to keep people coming back. MoneyUp exists to do the opposite. Who we're for: People who need access to small amounts of credit fast — and deserve to feel respected while getting it. They're not looking for a lecture. They're looking for speed, clarity, and a lender that doesn't feel like a last resort. Tone: Confident without being cocky. Warm without being patronizing. Honest about what we are and proud of it. Design principle: Every screen should feel like it was designed by someone who has been in this situation. Not someone looking at it from the outside. Success metric (Month 1): A client who completes their first MoneyUp loan in under 8 minutes and leaves a review without being asked.

COVID-19 Crisis Communications — Essential Business Transition
Communications

When the pandemic hit, Cashco served some of the most financially vulnerable Canadians — people who couldn't afford to wait. As part of the leadership team, I supported the development of COVID communications strategy — both internal and external — across 60+ branches: staff messaging, client-facing communications migrating services online, safety protocol rollouts, and executive updates to the board. The work had to be calm, clear, and fast — simultaneously reassuring staff and keeping clients served during the most chaotic stretch any of us had worked through.

Outcome Zero service gap during full branch lockdown. Remote transition completed within 72 hours. Zero documented client incidents attributable to communication failure. Best in class award received.

Sample — Client Email Communication, March 2020

Subject: An Important Update from Cashco Financial Dear Cashco Client, As the COVID-19 situation continues to evolve, the health and safety of our clients and staff remains our highest priority. Effective immediately, Cashco Financial is transitioning to a remote-only business model. Our branches are temporarily closed to walk-in traffic, and all client services will be available by telephone and email during regular business hours. Cashco has been deemed an essential service. We are committed to supporting you through this transition without interruption. Your loan associate remains available to assist you with: — New loan inquiries — Payment dates and payment handling — Re-loan requests Please contact your branch directly by phone or email, or reach out to our head office team with any questions or concerns. We are actively working alongside government directives and will continue to provide updates as further guidance emerges. We're here for you — now more than ever. The Cashco Financial Team
