I owned concept development, stakeholder buy-in, editorial strategy, design direction, and distribution.

Outcome National visibility for Cashco's mission. Thought-leadership credibility across the financial inclusion space. First publication of its kind in the alternative lending category in Canada.

Sample — Executive Summary Opening

The Pathway Report: What 150,000 Canadians Taught Us About Financial Exclusion Most financial institutions measure success in approvals. We started measuring it in pathways — the journeys our clients take from financial crisis to stability, and what actually makes those journeys shorter. Over two years, we analyzed behavioural patterns, sentiment data, and milestone outcomes across our client base. What we found challenges the conventional story about alternative lending. Our clients are not financial failures. They are people navigating gaps that the mainstream system wasn't built to handle — gaps in credit history, in documentation, in timing. When the right product meets the right moment, something changes: habits shift, credit improves, and dependence on high-cost credit shrinks. This report documents what that looks like, and what it means for the future of financial inclusion in Canada.

Sample — Brand Brief, MoneyUp

MoneyUp — Brand Brief The opportunity: The payday loan space has one experience: transactional, rushed, and designed to keep people coming back. MoneyUp exists to do the opposite. Who we're for: People who need access to small amounts of credit fast — and deserve to feel respected while getting it. They're not looking for a lecture. Design principle: Every screen should feel like it was designed by someone who has been in this situation. Not someone looking at it from the outside. Success metric (Month 1): A client who completes their first MoneyUp loan in under 8 minutes and leaves a review without being asked.
